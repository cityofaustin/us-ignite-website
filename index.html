Community+Tech’s Gigabit Competition encourages civic-minded hackers, entrepreneurs and thought leaders to come together to solve community problems with smart solutions. We are focused on:

Thoughtful Leadership

Austin is making intentional efforts to lead its rapidly growing technology sector toward social and local causes. This competition supports solutions for everyday citizens that are inclusive and responsive to the broader community needs.

Engaging the Community

We’re looking for civic-oriented tech experts to make local public information more personalized, usable and actionable for all Austinites. We want participants from all sectors to inform useful, usable solutions.

Human Centered

Why does gigabit technology even matter? Because it means a better, safer, more efficient Austin. We hope to connect everyday citizens with developers to come up with efficient, impactful solutions.

GigaTECH Competition Overview

Feb 23–
Apr 4

Ideation Phase

Civic-minded developers create application ideas to submit to a panel of judges. Competing teams will be matched with a volunteer mentor.

Rolling applications will be accepted until April 4, with mentors assigned to competitors based on relevance and date of submission. Application entries will be evaluated by a diverse panel of judges, who will select 8-10 finalists to move forward to the second round of competition on April 18.

Apr 18–
Jun 8

Prototype Iteration

After finalist teams are chosen, they work with their mentors and advisors to iterate application prototypes for approximately 11 weeks.

The University of Texas will host a GENI rack, provided by US Ignite, for teams to test their apps using a fast gigabit data connection. Finalists will present their progress to a new panel of judges in early June, who will select 2 winning ideas to receive funding.

Jun 8–
Dec 31

Build Apps

2 winning teams receive seed funding to finish their applications, to be ready for deployment by December 31st, 2017.

Both winning teams will demonstrate their apps at the Smart Cities Summit on June 26–28, and will build their app for deployment to the US Ignite Application Library.

Winners may earn a Grand Prize [$10,000] and Second Place Prize [$7,500] to fund the development of their innovative solution. Additional subject-matter prize awards may be granted.

Become a Mentor

We will connect our mentors to specific entrepreneurs to work through each stage of the competition. Through this relationship, mentors will help competitors refine their business ideas, develop prototypes and craft their final pitch.

Our goal is to leverage Austin’s gigabit internet technology to create smart solutions that engage the community. Do you think all day about health, education, transportation or clean energy? Your mentorship will marry some of the city’s biggest ideas with your real-life experience.

This is what a mentor is:

  • An advocate for their mentee
  • A competition subject-matter expert
  • An innovator or creative thinker
  • A leader and advisor
  • Interested in solving big problems

Get Involved

We are looking for partners and sponsors who are interested in supporting innovative solutions and technologies for the City of Austin and its everyday citizens.

Are you an interested in supporting Health, Education, Transportation or Clean Energy? Can you provide space, storage, new business services (legal, accounting, counseling), mentorship or a cash donation? We’d love to talk to you.
